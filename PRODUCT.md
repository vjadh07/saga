# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary users are hackathon judges and technical evaluators seeing Saga for the first time. They need to understand the product in less than one minute. Secondary users are writers, researchers, editors, and content teams reviewing AI-written drafts before publication.

## Product Purpose

Saga lets someone paste AI-written text, see what can be trusted, understand what needs revision and why, and receive a corrected draft with a verification receipt. The interface succeeds when a first-time user can state the audit outcome, identify risky claims, inspect supporting proof, and know what is safe to publish.

## Brand Personality

Forensic, assured, and candid. Saga should feel like a calm expert who shows its work, not a science-fiction AI assistant.

## Anti-references

- Dense internal diagnostics consoles
- Terminal cosplay and generic black-and-neon AI dashboards
- Unexplained internal terms such as "Trust Passport" or "Flight Recorder" as primary navigation
- Decorative glass, glow, and fake thinking animations
- Repetitive grids of equal-weight cards
- Technical metrics that hide the publishing decision

## Design Principles

1. Answer first. Lead with the publishing decision and the claims that need attention.
2. Use plain language first. Put technical terms in supporting details.
3. Show proof on demand. Keep evidence and provenance easy to reach without overwhelming the first screen.
4. Preserve user control. Make modes, selected claims, and next actions explicit.
5. Represent state honestly. Never blur Demo results with Live results or simulate provider activity.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA contrast targets, support keyboard navigation, never use color as the only status signal, respect reduced-motion preferences, use readable body text, preserve visible focus states, and make the full audit understandable on mobile.
